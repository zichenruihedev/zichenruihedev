10Regional Privacy Rights

10.1 European Union (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Request copies of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Request limitation of data processing
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time
  • Right to Lodge Complaint: File a complaint with data protection authorities

10.2 California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights:

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of data collection and sharing practices
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information
  • Right to Opt-Out: Opt out of the sale of personal information
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: Equal service regardless of exercising rights

We do not sell personal information as defined under CCPA. We may share data with advertising partners, which may constitute "sharing" under CCPA, for which you may opt out.

10.3 Other Regional Rights

Depending on your location, you may have additional rights under:

  • Brazil (LGPD): Rights similar to GDPR with local modifications
  • Canada (PIPEDA): Access, correction, and withdrawal rights
  • Australia (Privacy Act): Access and correction rights
  • Japan (APPI): Rights to disclosure, correction, and cessation of third-party sharing
  • South Korea (PIPA): Rights to access, correction, and deletion
  • United Kingdom (UK GDPR): Rights equivalent to EU GDPR post-Brexit

16Data Breach Notification

We have established procedures to respond to personal data breaches in accordance with applicable laws. In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify:

  • Affected Users: Without undue delay, providing clear and plain language explanation of the nature of the breach, likely consequences, and measures taken or proposed
  • Data Protection Authorities: Where required by law (within 72 hours under GDPR), we will notify the relevant supervisory authority
  • Law Enforcement: Where criminal activity is suspected, we will cooperate with appropriate authorities

Our Breach Response Process:

  • Immediate assessment and containment of the breach
  • Evaluation of the scope and severity of the breach
  • Notification to affected individuals within prescribed timeframes
  • Remediation measures and prevention of future incidents
  • Documentation of all breaches and responses
